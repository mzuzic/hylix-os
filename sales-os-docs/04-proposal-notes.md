# AI Sales OS — Proposal Notes (for the one-pager)

Critique of the original outline, to fix in the proposal draft.

## Keep

- Exec summary leads with the right outcome: less admin, more selling
- Capabilities framed as outcomes ("hands-free CRM updates")
- Flow: what it does → how → why it matters

## Fix

1. **Altitude** — cut the 5-pillar architecture to a 2-sentence "how it works".
   Local business owners care about hours saved, cost, time-to-live, data
   safety — not connectors and agents.
2. **Numbers** — add at least one quantified claim (e.g., "reps spend 30%+ of
   their week on admin"), time-to-live, and pricing.
3. **CTA** — end with a concrete next step ("book a 20-min demo" / "pilot live
   in 2 weeks"), not a value statement.
4. **Target** — narrow to local B2B services with 2+ reps, or reframe for solo
   operators. Current draft assumes a sales team many local businesses don't have.
5. **Trust** — one line on human-in-the-loop (all outbound emails/CRM writes
   are drafts for review) and where data lives (their own tools + their own
   Claude account; we never hold their credentials).
6. **Length** — current outline won't fit one page; let capabilities carry it.

## New selling points from scoping

- **Mobile**: briefs, dashboard, and voice updates from the truck/job site
  (Cowork mobile, July 2026). Arguably the headline for local businesses.
- **Their data stays theirs**: direct connectors, own OAuth, own subscription.
- **SOPs that improve monthly**: hosted SOP tools updated centrally — the
  retainer justification.

## Commercial shape (draft)

- Setup fee: connectors + Second Brain seeding + plugin install (~1–2 weeks)
- Monthly retainer: SOP updates, tune-ups, new skills
- Client pays own Claude subscription (Team/Max)
- Premium tier: server-side unattended automations
